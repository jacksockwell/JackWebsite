More about me!

I am a 3D artist who loves building character-driven work and pushing each project further than the last. A lot of what keeps me interested in art is the process itself: starting from a rough idea, finding the right shapes, and slowly turning that first idea into something that feels alive.

Most of my work lives somewhere between stylized character art, game-inspired design, and scene building. I enjoy projects where I can move between modeling, texturing, materials, lighting, and presentation instead of stopping at only one step. That full pipeline is one of the things that makes 3D so fun for me.

Blender and Substance Painter are the tools I spend the most time in, and I like using them to explore personality in a model through costume details, shape language, and surface wear. I am also interested in the Unreal Engine side of the process, especially how characters and assets fit into a bigger game workflow once the art is finished.

A lot of the pieces I make start as fan art or concept-driven ideas because they give me a chance to study mood, storytelling, and visual style while still putting my own spin on them. I like art that feels memorable, a little strange, and full of intention, whether that comes through a character design, a prop, or the lighting in a final scene.

I want the work on this site to show not just finished renders, but the range of things I enjoy making and the way I think through a project from start to finish. I like projects that let me experiment, solve visual problems, and keep improving the quality of every piece I put out.

Right now I want to keep growing as an artist, keep sharpening my character work, and keep taking on projects that help me get faster, better, and more creative. Whether it is freelance work, collaboration, or personal pieces, I want every project to teach me something new and leave me with stronger work than the last one.
